New Vulnerabilities Disclosed in React Server Components
The React team has disclosed two new vulnerabilities affecting React Server Components: a high-severity Denial of Service (DoS) and a medium-severity Source Code Exposure. The DoS flaw allows a malicious HTTP request to trigger an infinite loop that hangs the server process. The other vulnerability can be used to expose the source code of server-side functions. These flaws do not permit remote code execution, but an immediate upgrade to patched versions is recommended for all affected frameworks and packages, including Next.js.

Anthropic Researcher: Core AI Alignment Problems Remain Unsolved
An Anthropic researcher argues that despite current AI models appearing "pretty well aligned," the fundamental challenges of AI safety remain unsolved. He contends that the hardest problems, such as overseeing superhuman systems (outer alignment) and preventing deceptive goal-seeking (inner alignment), have not yet been truly tested in practice. While current models are manageable, he warns against complacency, stating that future systems trained on long-horizon, real-world tasks are likely to develop dangerous, power-seeking behaviors that will be much harder to detect and control.

New Open-Source Tools for AI and Data Development
Two new open-source projects have been released for developers:
* Sim: A platform for visually building and deploying AI agent workflows. It uses a drag-and-drop canvas to connect agents and tools, supports knowledge bases, and can be self-hosted with local models via Ollama.
* Stoolap: An embedded SQL database written in Rust. It features both in-memory and persistent storage, full ACID compliance, multi-version concurrency control (MVCC), and time-travel queries for accessing historical data.

Science & Environment

CRISPR-Edited Fungus Offers Sustainable, High-Protein Meat Alternative
Scientists have used CRISPR gene-editing to create a more nutritious and sustainable strain of the fungus Fusarium venenatum, which is already approved as a meat alternative. By modifying two genes, researchers made the fungal protein easier for humans to digest and significantly improved production efficiency—requiring 44% less sugar and producing protein 88% faster. The new strain shows a substantially lower environmental footprint than conventional livestock, using 70% less land than chicken production and reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

The "Wonder Material" Enabling a Revolution in Medical Imaging
Cadmium zinc telluride (CZT), a specialized semiconductor, is driving significant advances in medical imaging, airport security, and scientific research. The material allows scanners to detect X-ray and gamma-ray photons with exceptional precision, resulting in faster, more detailed 3D scans with lower radiation doses. A new scanner using CZT at a London hospital has reduced lung scan times from 45 minutes to just 15. However, CZT is extremely difficult and slow to manufacture, with only a few global suppliers, creating a bottleneck for its many applications.

Study Finds AI Agents May Amplify Human Inequality
A new paper examining negotiations conducted by AI agents on behalf of humans found that this delegation led to greater, rather than reduced, variation in economic outcomes. The success of an AI agent was significantly influenced by the characteristics and personality of its human creator, suggesting human traits are inherited and transformed by AI proxies. The study also identifies "machine fluency"—the skill of effectively instructing an AI—as a potential new source of economic inequality in an "agentic economy."

Politics & Society

Mass Crime, Not Racism, Was Primary Driver of Mass Incarceration, Paper Argues
A new paper challenges the view that racism was the primary cause of mass incarceration in the United States. The research posits that the main driver was the dramatic rise in violent crime from the 1960s through the 1980s. Evidence from public opinion data and legislative voting records indicates that support for "tough on crime" policies was widespread across racial lines. Black communities and their political leaders also advocated for more punitive measures in response to the disproportionate impact of crime on their constituents.
